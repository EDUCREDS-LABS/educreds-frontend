1. Purpose of the Agreement
This Agreement governs the Institution’s participation in the EduCreds Protocol as an authorized credential issuer, including:

Issuance of academic credentials

Use of blockchain-based NFTs and W3C Verifiable Credentials

Compliance with governance, AI-based monitoring, and dispute resolution mechanisms

2. Definitions
Credential: A non-transferable (soulbound) digital academic certificate issued via EduCreds.

Institution Identity NFT (IIN): A soulbound NFT representing the Institution’s verified on-chain identity.

PoIC (Proof of Institutional Credibility): A dynamic reputation score assigned to institutions.

DAO: EduCreds decentralized governance body.

Representative (Rep): A wallet authorized by the Institution to issue credentials.

3. Institution Onboarding & Verification
3.1 The Institution agrees that onboarding requires submission of accurate legal, academic, and operational information.

3.2 EduCreds may utilize automated analysis tools (including AI systems) to evaluate legitimacy, risk, and credibility.

3.3 Final authorization is granted only after DAO approval, upon which an Institution Identity NFT (IIN) is minted.

4. Rights Granted to the Institution
Upon approval, the Institution is granted:

The right to issue credentials via EduCreds smart contracts

The right to designate authorized representatives

Participation rights in protocol governance (weighted by PoIC score)

5. Credential Issuance Rules
The Institution agrees that:

5.1 All credentials issued must represent truthful, verifiable academic achievements.

5.2 Credentials are:

Non-transferable (soulbound)

Permanently recorded on-chain

Publicly verifiable

5.3 Issuance limits may apply based on:

Subscription tier

Governance decisions

PoIC score

5.4 Credential revocation is permitted only for legitimate reasons, including fraud, error, or dispute resolution outcomes.

6. Representatives & Access Control
6.1 The Institution may authorize specific wallet addresses as Representatives.

6.2 The Institution is fully responsible for actions taken by its Representatives.

6.3 Compromised or abusive Representative access must be revoked immediately.

7. Proof of Institutional Credibility (PoIC)
7.1 PoIC is not an NFT and is not transferable.

7.2 PoIC scores are calculated dynamically based on:

Issuance accuracy

Revocation behavior

Employer and verifier feedback

Governance participation

AI risk assessments

7.3 PoIC scores may increase or decrease automatically.

7.4 PoIC impacts:

Governance voting weight

Issuance privileges

Risk monitoring thresholds

8. Monitoring, Audits & AI Analysis
8.1 The Institution acknowledges that EduCreds employs:

Automated monitoring systems

Pattern analysis

AI-assisted risk detection

8.2 Abnormal behavior (e.g., issuance spikes, fraud signals) may trigger:

Temporary suspension

Governance review

Formal disputes

9. Disputes & Enforcement
9.1 Disputes are resolved through on-chain governance voting.

9.2 Possible enforcement outcomes include:

Credential revocation

Temporary suspension of issuance rights

Permanent revocation of the Institution Identity NFT

9.3 Governance execution is final and binding.

10. Prohibited Conduct
The Institution agrees not to:

Issue credentials without academic basis

Sell credentials

Misrepresent institutional authority

Attempt to manipulate governance or PoIC scoring

Bypass issuance limits or access controls

11. Liability & Indemnification
11.1 EduCreds provides infrastructure “as-is” without warranties.

11.2 The Institution bears full responsibility for:

Credential content

Academic legitimacy

Compliance with local laws

11.3 The Institution agrees to indemnify EduCreds from claims arising from misuse or misrepresentation.

12. Termination
12.1 This Agreement may be terminated if:

The Institution’s IIN is revoked

Fraud or misconduct is proven

Required compliance is breached

12.2 Termination does not erase previously issued credentials, which remain verifiable but may be marked invalid.

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of [Jurisdiction], without prejudice to decentralized governance enforcement.
